Famous Inhabitants of Leuven

Leuven has a lot of famous inhabitants. In what follows some of them:

Milow

  • °1981
  • singer-songwriter
  • started his music career at humo’s Rock Rally in 2004, where he reached the finals.
  • was the major winner of the Music industry Awards (MiAs) in 2007, 2008 and 2011.

Dries Mertens

  • °1987
  • football player of the Belgian national team
  • gained his first experience as a footballer at the youth setup of stade Leuven.
  • in late 2015, Dries and his wife bought the 16th-century building De Dry Coningen, a monument where mayors and dignitaries of Leuven once lived.

Andreas Vesalius

  • °1514 †1564
  • revolutionised human anatomy with his masterwork De humani corporis fabrica
  • classically trained in Leuven where he graduated in medicine in 1537
  • Vesalius dissected as first man human bodies

Georges Lemaître

  • °1894 †1966
  • astronomer and professor of physics at the Catholic University of Leuven
  • proposed the Big Bang theory of the origin of the universe
  • was introduced to the University's Burroughs E 101, its first electronic computer (1958)

Facts and Figures

  • Every year in september, the population of over 98,000 inhabitants grows by 51,000 future bachelors and masters.
  • Leuven is home to no less than 155 different nationalities.
  • Every year, some 1,200,000 meals are sold in the Almas (student restaurants) of Leuven.
  • With as many as 182 restaurants and 240 pubs, the city centre of Leuven probably has the largest concentration of catering establishments in Flanders.
  • The diameter of the city centre, measured on the inside of the ringroad around the city, is approximately 2 km.
